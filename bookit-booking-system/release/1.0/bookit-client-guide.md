# Bookit — Your Complete Business Guide

Welcome to Bookit. This guide covers everything you need to run your bookings day to day — from setting up your services to understanding what your customers see when they book with you.

---

## 1. Welcome and Getting Started

Bookit gives your business a professional online booking page that your customers can use at any time, and a separate dashboard where you manage everything. Customers book and pay online in minutes. You see every appointment in one place, get notified automatically, and never have to chase a booking by phone.

### Logging into your dashboard

Your dashboard lives at a web address that looks like this:

```
yourdomain.co.uk/bookit-dashboard
```

Your Wimbledon Smart installer will give you the exact address, along with your email address and a starting password. Go to that address in any browser, enter your email and password, and click **Log In**.

> **Tip:** Bookmark the dashboard address so you can get back to it quickly.

### The first-time setup wizard

The first time you log in, Bookit will guide you through a short setup process. It has four steps:

1. **Add your first service** — Give it a name (e.g. "Deep Tissue Massage"), a duration, and a price.
2. **Set your availability** — Choose which days and hours you're open.
3. **Configure payments** — Your card payment details will already be set up by your installer. This step confirms everything looks right.
4. **Start taking bookings** — You'll see a summary and a link to your booking page.

Once you click **Go to Dashboard**, the wizard closes and won't appear again. You can change everything it set up at any time from your dashboard.

### What each menu item does

Once you're in the dashboard, you'll see a menu on the left side:

- **Bookings** — Your calendar and list of all appointments
- **Services** — The treatments or sessions customers can book
- **Staff** — Your team members and their working hours
- **Customers** — Everyone who has ever booked with you
- **Packages** — Prepaid session bundles
- **Reports** — Revenue, booking trends, and staff performance
- **Settings** — Business information, payment setup, email preferences, and cancellation policy

### Logging out

Click your name or photo in the top corner of the dashboard and select **Log Out**.

## 2. Managing Your Services

A service is anything a customer can book — for example, "Women's Haircut", "60-Minute Massage", or "Initial Consultation". Every booking is tied to a service.

### Adding a new service

1. Go to **Services** in the left menu.
2. Click **Add Service**.
3. Fill in the details:
   - **Service name** — What the customer sees when booking (e.g. "Swedish Massage")
   - **Description** — Optional. Useful for explaining what's included.
   - **Duration** — How long the appointment lasts, in minutes.
   - **Price** — The full price of the service.
   - **Buffer time** — An optional gap after the appointment before the next one can start (useful for cleaning up or travel).
   - **Deposit** — Choose whether customers pay the full amount at booking, a fixed deposit (e.g. £20), or a percentage (e.g. 25%). You can also set no deposit at all, which means payment is on arrival.
4. Click **Save**.

The service is now active and will appear in your booking page immediately.

### Service categories

Categories help organise your services for customers. For example, you might have categories like "Hair", "Nails", and "Treatments". Customers see services grouped by category when they book.

To add a category, go to **Services**, click **Categories**, and click **Add Category**. You can then assign services to that category when creating or editing them.

### Making a service active or inactive

If you want to stop taking bookings for a service temporarily — for example, during a quiet season — you can make it inactive without deleting it. Go to the service in your list, click **Edit**, and toggle the **Active** switch off. Inactive services don't appear in your booking page but all their history is kept.

### Editing and reordering services

Click **Edit** next to any service to update its name, price, duration, or any other detail. To change the order services appear in for customers, drag them into position in the services list.

## 3. Managing Your Staff

Each person who takes bookings in Bookit has their own staff profile. Customers can choose a specific person or select "No Preference" to be assigned whoever is available.

### Adding a new staff member

1. Go to **Staff** in the left menu.
2. Click **Add Staff Member**.
3. Fill in their details:
   - **First and last name**
   - **Email address** — This is what they'll use to log into the dashboard.
   - **Phone number**
   - **Job title** — Optional, but shown to customers when they choose a staff member.
   - **Bio** — Optional. A short description of their experience or specialisms.
   - **Photo** — Optional. Customers will see this when choosing staff.
4. Assign the **services** they offer by ticking the relevant boxes.
5. Set their **working hours** (see below).
6. Click **Add Staff Member**.

They'll receive a welcome email with their login details and a link to the dashboard.

### Setting which services each staff member offers

When adding or editing a staff member, you'll see a list of all your services with checkboxes. Only tick the services that person delivers. They'll only appear as an option for customers booking those services.

You can also set a different price for that staff member on a specific service — for example, if a senior stylist charges more than a junior one.

### Setting working hours

Under the **Working Hours** section of a staff member's profile, you can set which days they work and between what times. You can add more than one time block per day — this is useful if someone works a split shift, such as 9am–1pm and then 3pm–7pm with a lunch break in between.

Toggle any day off if they don't work that day.

### Blocking time off

To block out holidays, a one-off personal appointment, or any other time when a staff member is unavailable, go to their profile and use the **Block Time Off** section. You can block a single day, a range of dates, or a specific time on a specific day.

Customers will not see those slots as available during the blocked period.

### Notification preferences

Each staff member can choose how they receive notifications about new bookings:

- **Immediate** — An email is sent the moment a booking is assigned to them.
- **Daily digest** — One summary email per day listing all new bookings.
- **Weekly digest** — One summary email per week.
- **Daily schedule email** — A morning email with that day's appointments listed.

You can set these preferences on behalf of a staff member from their profile, and they can also set their own preferences from their own dashboard login.

### Making a staff member active or inactive

If someone leaves or goes on extended leave, you can deactivate their account rather than deleting it. Go to their profile and toggle their status to **Inactive**. They won't appear in the booking flow and can't log into the dashboard. Their previous booking history is preserved.

## 4. Managing Bookings

### Viewing today's schedule

When you log in, your dashboard home shows **Today's Appointments** — a quick list of everything happening today, in time order. You can see the customer name, service, time, and status at a glance.

### Calendar view vs list view

**Calendar view** gives you a visual overview. You can switch between Day, Week, and Month. Bookings appear as colour-coded blocks:

- **Blue** — Confirmed
- **Green** — Completed
- **Yellow** — Pending payment
- **Red** — Cancelled
- **Grey** — No-show

**List view** gives you a sortable, filterable table of bookings. This is useful for searching by customer name, checking a date range, or exporting records.

Use calendar view to get the big picture. Use list view when you need to find a specific booking or see a filtered set.

### Filtering bookings

In both views, you can filter by:

- **Date or date range**
- **Staff member**
- **Service**
- **Status** (confirmed, completed, cancelled, etc.)

### Creating a manual booking

If a customer calls or walks in and you want to record their booking in the system:

1. Click **+ Create Manual Booking**.
2. Search for the customer by name or email. If they're new, select **Create New Customer** and enter their details.
3. Choose the service.
4. Choose the staff member.
5. Pick the date and time.
6. Choose the payment method — card (if paying now), or pay on arrival.
7. Click **Confirm Booking**.

The customer will receive a confirmation email exactly as if they had booked online.

### Editing a booking

Click on any booking to open its details, then click **Edit**. You can change the date, time, staff member, or service. The customer will be notified of the change by email.

### Marking a booking as complete or no-show

Click on a booking and use the quick action buttons:

- **Mark Complete** — Use this after the appointment has finished.
- **Mark No-Show** — Use this if the customer didn't turn up.

These statuses feed into your reports.

### Cancelling a booking

To cancel a booking from the dashboard:

1. Open the booking.
2. Click **Cancel Booking**.
3. Confirm the cancellation.

If the customer paid by card, Bookit will automatically process a refund to their card. The customer will receive a cancellation confirmation email.

### Understanding booking statuses

- **Pending** — The booking has been started but not yet confirmed (e.g. the customer hasn't completed payment).
- **Pending Payment** — The customer chose to pay by card but hasn't completed the payment yet.
- **Confirmed** — The booking is active and the appointment is scheduled.
- **Completed** — The appointment has taken place and been marked as done.
- **Cancelled** — The booking was cancelled, either by the customer, a staff member, or you.
- **No-show** — The customer didn't attend and the appointment was marked accordingly.

## 5. Payments and Packages

### How card payments work

When a customer pays by card, they enter their details on a secure payment page (provided by Stripe, a leading UK payment processor). You never see their card details — only the amount and the confirmation that payment was successful. The money is deposited into your account by Stripe, typically within two business days.

### How pay on arrival works

Some customers may prefer to pay in person when they arrive for their appointment. When they choose this option at booking, no payment is taken online. The booking is confirmed, and you collect the payment at the appointment.

### Marking a pay-on-arrival booking as paid

When you collect payment in person, you can record it in the dashboard:

1. Open the booking.
2. Click **Record Payment**.
3. Enter the amount and how it was paid (cash, card machine, bank transfer).
4. Click **Save**.

This keeps your records accurate and lets reports reflect actual revenue.

### What session packages are

Packages let customers buy a bundle of sessions upfront at a discounted price — for example, "5 massages for £200" instead of paying £50 each time. The customer buys the package once, then redeems one session from it each time they book.

Packages are great for building customer loyalty and securing advance revenue.

### Creating and managing package types

1. Go to **Packages** in the left menu.
2. Click **Add Package Type**.
3. Give it a name (e.g. "5-Session Bundle"), set how many sessions it includes, and set the price.
4. Save.

Customers will see this package as an option during booking when they're choosing how to pay.

### Viewing a customer's packages

Go to **Customers**, find the customer, and open their profile. You'll see a **Packages** section listing any packages they've purchased, how many sessions remain, and the history of which appointments each session was used for.

You can cancel a customer's package from this view if needed.

## 6. Your Customers

### Viewing the customer list

Go to **Customers** in the left menu. You'll see a table of everyone who has ever booked with your business. You can search by name, email, or phone number, and filter by how recently they visited or how many bookings they've made.

### Viewing a customer's profile

Click any customer's name to open their profile. You'll see:

- Their contact details
- Total number of bookings and total amount spent
- Their last visit date
- A full history of every appointment
- Any packages they've bought and how many sessions remain
- A record of all payments

### Changing a customer's email address

If a customer asks you to update the email address on their account:

1. Open their profile.
2. Click **Edit**.
3. Update the email field and save.

The customer will receive an email at their new address to confirm the change. Their login will be updated automatically.

### Exporting a customer's data

If a customer asks for a copy of all the data you hold about them (this is their legal right under UK data protection law):

1. Open their profile.
2. Click **Export Data**.

Bookit will generate a file containing all their personal information and booking history, which you can send to them.

### Deleting a customer record

If a customer asks to be removed from your records (also their legal right):

1. Open their profile.
2. Click **Delete Customer**.
3. Confirm.

Bookit will remove all personal details — name, email, phone number, and any notes. Their booking records are kept, but shown as "Deleted Customer" with no personal information attached. This is required by UK law: financial records must be kept for seven years for tax purposes.

> **Important:** If the customer has any upcoming bookings, you'll need to cancel those first.

## 7. Email Notifications

### What emails are sent to customers automatically

Bookit sends these emails to customers without you having to do anything:

- **Booking confirmation** — Sent immediately after they book. Contains full appointment details, a button to add it to their calendar, a link to cancel, and a link to reschedule.
- **24-hour reminder** — Sent automatically the day before their appointment.
- **Cancellation confirmation** — Sent if they cancel (or if you cancel the booking from the dashboard).
- **Reschedule confirmation** — Sent when an appointment is moved to a new date or time.

### What the confirmation email contains

The confirmation email gives the customer everything they need:

- Date, time, service, and staff member name
- Your business name and address
- **Add to Calendar** button (works with Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, and Outlook)
- **Cancel Booking** link — takes them directly to a cancellation page
- **Reschedule** link — takes them to a page where they can pick a new time

### How customers cancel or reschedule using the email links

When a customer clicks **Cancel Booking** in their email, they are taken to a cancellation page and asked to confirm. Once they confirm, the cancellation is processed and they receive a confirmation email. If they paid by card, they receive an automatic refund (see your cancellation policy in Settings for the rules).

When a customer clicks the reschedule link, they see their current booking and a calendar to choose a new slot. Once they pick a new time, the change is made immediately and both they and the relevant staff member are notified.

### What emails your staff receive

Your staff members receive emails when:

- A new booking is assigned to them
- A booking they're assigned to is rescheduled
- A booking they're assigned to is cancelled
- They are reassigned to a booking that was previously with someone else
- Their daily schedule (if they've enabled this option in their profile)

### Checking whether an email was sent

If you ever need to check whether a specific email was sent to a customer or staff member:

1. Go to **Settings**.
2. Click the **Email Queue** tab.

You'll see a log of every email Bookit has sent, including the status (sent, failed, or pending) and the date and time it was sent.

## 8. Settings

### Business information

Go to **Settings** and update your:

- Business name
- Address
- Phone number
- Business email address

These details appear in customer emails and on your booking page.

### Cancellation policy

The cancellation policy sets how much notice a customer must give to cancel or reschedule without penalty. For example, if your window is 24 hours, customers can cancel up to 24 hours before their appointment and receive a full refund. Inside that window, the cancellation is blocked — they'll see a message explaining they can no longer cancel online and should contact you directly.

To set the window: go to **Settings** → **Cancellation Policy** and choose the notice period from the dropdown.

### Email settings

Under **Settings** → **Email**, you can set:

- **From name** — The name customers see in the "From" field of your emails (e.g. your business name).
- **From email** — The address your emails come from (must be set up by your installer).

### Turning session packages on or off

If you'd rather not offer session packages, you can switch them off entirely. Go to **Settings** and toggle **Packages** off. They will disappear from the booking wizard and from your dashboard menus until you turn them back on.

## 9. Understanding What Your Customers See

### The 5 steps of the booking wizard

When a customer visits your booking page, they go through five steps:

1. **Choose a service** — They pick from your list of active services, grouped by category.
2. **Choose a staff member** — They select a specific person or choose "No Preference" to be matched with whoever is available.
3. **Choose a date and time** — They see a calendar showing only real available slots based on the staff member's working hours and existing bookings. UK bank holidays are blocked automatically.
4. **Enter their details and pay** — They fill in their name, email, and phone number, then choose how to pay: by card (charged now) or pay on arrival.
5. **Confirmation** — They see a confirmation screen with their booking details and are told a confirmation email is on its way.

If a customer has a session package, they can redeem a session from it at step 5 instead of paying again.

### The confirmation page

After booking, the customer lands on a confirmation page showing:

- The service, date, time, and staff member
- A summary of what they paid (or that they're paying on arrival)
- A reminder that a confirmation email has been sent

### The confirmation email

The email arrives within a minute or two of booking. It contains all the booking details, a calendar button, a cancel link, and a reschedule link. Customers can come back to this email at any point to manage their appointment.

### How cancellation and rescheduling work from the customer's side

Customers don't need to log in or create an account to cancel or reschedule. The links in their confirmation email take them directly to a page where they can make changes with one click.

### What happens if a customer tries to cancel inside the policy window

If a customer clicks the cancel link in their email but their appointment is within your cancellation window (e.g. less than 24 hours away), they'll see a message explaining that cancellations are no longer accepted online at this point and that they should contact you directly. The cancellation will not go through automatically.
