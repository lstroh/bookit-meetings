## 2. What Personal Data We Collect and Why

We collect personal data when you make or manage a booking through our online
booking system (the Bookit system), when you contact us, and when you use our
website.

### 2.1 Booking and account data

When you make a booking, we collect:

- Your first name and last name
- Your email address
- Your telephone number
- Details of the service booked (type, date, time, and staff member)
- Your booking status and history
- Any special requests or notes you provide
- If you purchase a session package: the package details and redemption history

**Why:** We need this information to take your booking, confirm it, send you
reminders, and deliver the service. The lawful basis for this processing is
**contract performance** (UK GDPR Article 6(1)(b)) — we cannot provide the
service without it.

### 2.2 Payment data

When you pay by card, payment is processed by Stripe, our payment provider.
We record:

- The amount paid
- The payment method (card or pay on arrival)
- A transaction reference (Stripe session ID)

**We do not store your full card number, expiry date, or CVV.** These are
handled entirely by Stripe and never pass through our systems.

**Why:** To process your payment and maintain financial records. The lawful
basis is **contract performance** (Article 6(1)(b)) for payment processing,
and **legal obligation** (Article 6(1)(c)) for retaining financial records
under HMRC requirements.

### 2.3 Cooling-off waiver

When you complete a booking for an appointment at a specific date and time,
you are asked to confirm that you waive your 14-day statutory cancellation
right under the Consumer Contracts (Information, Cancellation and Additional
Charges) Regulations 2013. We record the date and time you accepted this
waiver.

**Why:** To comply with consumer protection law. The lawful basis is
**legal obligation** (Article 6(1)(c)).

### 2.4 Technical and security data

Our booking system temporarily records IP addresses for the purpose of
rate-limiting (preventing abuse). This data is transient and is not retained
beyond 24 hours.

**Why:** To protect the security and integrity of our booking system. The
lawful basis is **legitimate interests** (Article 6(1)(f)) — specifically,
our legitimate interest in preventing fraud and system abuse.

## 5. Who We Share Your Data With

We do not sell your personal data. We share your data only with the following
trusted third-party processors, each of whom is bound by a Data Processing
Agreement (DPA) and processes data only as we instruct.

### 5.1 Brevo (Sendinblue SA)

**Role:** Transactional email delivery — sends your booking confirmation,
reminder, and update emails on our behalf.

**Data shared:** Your name and email address, and the content of booking
notification emails.

**Location:** Brevo is based in the EU and processes data within the EU/EEA,
which provides an equivalent level of protection to the UK.

**DPA:** brevo.com/legal/termsandconditions/

### 5.2 Stripe

**Role:** Payment processing — handles all card payments securely.

**Data shared:** Your name and email address, and payment transaction details.
Your full card details are handled by Stripe directly and are never shared
with us.

**Location:** Stripe operates within the EU/UK under appropriate data
protection frameworks.

**DPA:** stripe.com/en-gb/legal/dpa

### 5.3 Google (Google Calendar API) — if applicable

**Role:** Calendar synchronisation — if your appointment is synced to a staff
member's Google Calendar, limited booking details are shared with Google for
that purpose only.

**Data shared:** Appointment date, time, service name, and your name.

**Location:** Google operates under its Google Cloud Data Processing Addendum,
which provides appropriate safeguards.

**DPA:** cloud.google.com/terms/data-processing-addendum

*Note: Google Calendar sync is only used if we have enabled this feature.
If we have not, no data is shared with Google through this system.*

### 5.4 Hostinger (hosting provider)

**Role:** Our website and booking system are hosted on Hostinger's
infrastructure.

**Location:** Hostinger is based in Lithuania (EU) and stores data on EU
servers. The EU is recognised as providing an adequate level of data
protection under UK data protection law.

## 8. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

Under the UK GDPR, the Data Protection Act 2018, and the Data (Use and
Access) Act 2025, you have the following rights in relation to your personal
data.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at [BUSINESS EMAIL].
We will respond within one month of receiving your request.

### 8.1 Right to Access

You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you,
along with information about how we use it. This is sometimes called a
Subject Access Request (SAR).

### 8.2 Right to Rectification

You have the right to ask us to correct any inaccurate personal data we hold
about you. If you need to update your email address, please contact us
directly at [BUSINESS EMAIL]. We will process the change on your behalf and
send a verification link to your new email address to confirm the change
before it takes effect.

### 8.3 Right to Erasure (Right to Be Forgotten)

You have the right to ask us to delete your personal data. We will do so
unless we are required to retain it for legal reasons (for example, where
it forms part of a financial record we must keep for HMRC compliance). In
those cases, we will tell you what we are retaining and why.

### 8.4 Right to Restrict Processing

You have the right to ask us to pause or limit how we use your data —
for example, while we verify an accuracy dispute. During this period, we
will not use your data for any purpose other than storage.

### 8.5 Right to Data Portability

You have the right to receive a copy of your personal data in a structured,
commonly used, machine-readable format (such as CSV or JSON), and to transfer
it to another service provider where technically feasible.

### 8.6 Right to Object

You have the right to object to processing based on our legitimate interests
(see Section 3). If you object, we will stop processing unless we have
compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests, or the processing
is necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims.

### 8.7 Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making

We do not use automated decision-making or profiling that produces legal or
similarly significant effects on you.

### 8.8 How to Exercise Your Rights

Contact us at [BUSINESS EMAIL] with your request. We will respond within
**one calendar month**. In complex cases, we may extend this by a further
two months and will notify you if this is necessary.

We will not charge a fee for handling your request unless it is manifestly
unfounded or excessive.

## 9. Cookies

Our booking system uses session cookies strictly necessary to maintain the
state of your booking as you move through the booking wizard (for example,
to remember your selected service and time slot between steps). These cookies
are deleted when you close your browser or complete your booking.

**Strictly necessary cookies do not require your consent** under the UK
Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR).

We do not use analytics, advertising, or tracking cookies through the booking
system itself. If the wider website uses such cookies, this will be covered
by a separate cookie policy.

## 10. How We Keep Your Data Secure

We take reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your
personal data, including:

- **Encryption in transit:** All data is transmitted over encrypted HTTPS
  connections (TLS 1.2 or higher).
- **Encryption at rest:** Sensitive data, including any OAuth tokens used
  for Google Calendar integration, is encrypted at rest.
- **Password security:** Passwords are stored using secure cryptographic
  hashing and are never stored in plain text.
- **Access controls:** Personal data is accessible only to authorised
  staff members who need it to provide the service.
- **Hosting security:** Our booking system is hosted on Hostinger's
  EU infrastructure, which provides a secure and monitored environment.

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk
to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the ICO within 72 hours and
notify you without undue delay.
