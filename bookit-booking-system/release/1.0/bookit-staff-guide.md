# Bookit — Staff Guide

This guide covers everything you need to know to use Bookit day to day. It's short — your slice of the system is focused, and most of what you'll need becomes second nature quickly.

---

## 1. Welcome

Bookit is the booking system your business uses to take appointments online. When a customer books a session with you, it appears in your dashboard automatically. No phone calls to take, no paper diaries to update — it's all here.

Your dashboard shows your own bookings, lets you manage your availability, and keeps you notified of anything that changes. You won't see business-wide revenue figures or other staff members' customer details — your view is focused on your own schedule.

### Logging in

Your manager will send you a welcome email with your dashboard address and a temporary password. Go to that address in any browser, enter your email and the temporary password, and you'll be prompted to set a new one on your first login.

Your login email is the same address where you receive booking notifications — keep it somewhere handy.

> **Tip:** Bookmark your dashboard address so you can get back to it quickly from your phone or laptop.

## 2. Your Schedule

### Viewing today's appointments

When you log in, the first thing you see is **Today's Appointments** — a list of everything you have on today, in time order. Each entry shows:

- The appointment time and duration
- The customer's name
- The service
- The booking status (confirmed, completed, no-show)
- A badge if the customer still owes a balance to pay on arrival

### Viewing your upcoming schedule

Go to **My Schedule** in the left menu to see your appointments for the week ahead. You can switch between a calendar view and a list view — use whichever suits you best.

### What each booking status means

- **Confirmed** — The appointment is booked and active.
- **Completed** — The appointment has been marked as finished.
- **No-show** — The customer didn't turn up.
- **Cancelled** — The booking was cancelled, either by the customer or your manager.
- **Pending** — The booking is in progress but not yet fully confirmed (rare — usually resolves within a minute).

### Marking a booking as complete or no-show

Open any booking and use the quick action buttons:

- **Mark Complete** — Use this once the appointment has finished.
- **Mark No-Show** — Use this if the customer didn't attend.

If your manager has enabled earnings visibility for your account, these actions feed into your personal stats.

## 3. Managing Your Availability

### Setting your regular working hours

Go to **My Availability** in the left menu. You'll see your weekly schedule — the days you work and the hours you're available for bookings each day.

To change your hours:

1. Click the day you want to update.
2. Adjust the start and end times.
3. If you work a split shift (for example, mornings and evenings with a break in between), click **Add Hours** to add a second time block for that day.
4. Toggle any day off if you don't work it.
5. Save.

Changes take effect immediately — any slots that fall outside your new hours will no longer appear as available to customers.

### Blocking time off

If you need to block out a holiday, a personal appointment, a lunch break, or any other time when you're not available:

1. Go to **My Availability**.
2. Click **Block Time Off**.
3. Choose whether you're blocking a single time slot, a whole day, or a range of dates.
4. Add an optional note (e.g. "Annual leave") — this is just for your own reference.
5. Save.

Customers will not see those slots as available during the blocked period. Your manager can also see that the time is blocked.

## 4. Your Profile

Go to **My Profile** from the left menu to update your personal details.

### What you can update

- **Name** — Your first and last name as it appears to customers when they choose a staff member.
- **Photo** — A profile photo that customers see during booking. Recommended: a square image, clearly lit.
- **Bio** — A short description of your experience or specialisms. Optional, but a good bio can help customers choose you.
- **Job title** — Your role (e.g. "Senior Massage Therapist", "Physiotherapist").
- **Phone number** — For internal use by your manager; not shown to customers.

### Changing your password

In **My Profile**, scroll to the **Change Password** section. Enter your current password, then your new one twice, and save. Passwords must be at least 8 characters and include a mix of uppercase, lowercase, and numbers.

### Setting your notification preferences

You can choose how you want to be notified about new and changed bookings. Go to **My Profile** and look for the **Notifications** section:

- **Immediate** — You receive an email as soon as a booking is assigned to you, rescheduled, or cancelled. Best if you want to know straight away.
- **Daily digest** — One email per day summarising all new bookings from that day. Good if you prefer not to be interrupted throughout the day.
- **Weekly digest** — One email per week listing all bookings from that week. Useful if your schedule changes infrequently.
- **Daily schedule email** — A separate morning email listing just today's appointments. You can enable this alongside any of the options above.

## 5. Notifications You Will Receive

Bookit sends you email notifications automatically. The address used is the same one you log in with. Here's what to expect:

- **New booking** — Sent when a booking is assigned to you. Contains the customer name, service, date, and time.
- **Booking rescheduled** — Sent when one of your bookings is moved to a new date or time. The email shows both the old and new slot.
- **Booking cancelled** — Sent when a booking assigned to you is cancelled, whether by the customer or your manager. The appointment is removed from your schedule.
- **Reassigned booking** — Sent if a booking is moved from another staff member to you, or moved away from you to someone else.
- **Daily schedule** — If you've enabled this in your notification preferences, you'll receive a morning email with a list of today's appointments.

You don't need to take any action on most of these — they're there to keep you informed. If something looks wrong (for example, a cancellation you weren't expecting), contact your manager.

## 6. Google Calendar (if connected)

If you connect your Google Calendar to Bookit, your appointments will appear there automatically — alongside your personal events on your phone, laptop, or tablet.

### How to connect

1. Go to **My Profile**.
2. Scroll to the **Google Calendar** section.
3. Click **Connect Google Calendar**.
4. A Google sign-in window will open. Sign in with the Google account you want to use.
5. Approve the permission request (Bookit only asks for access to create and manage calendar events — it cannot read your other calendar events).
6. Once connected, you'll see a confirmation showing which Google account is linked.

From that point on, new bookings assigned to you will appear in your Google Calendar automatically. If a booking is rescheduled or cancelled, your calendar event is updated or removed to match.

> **Note:** Your manager needs to add your Google account as an authorised user before you can connect. If you see an "Access blocked" error during the process, let your manager know — it's a quick fix on their end.

### How to disconnect

Go to **My Profile** → **Google Calendar** → **Disconnect**. Your calendar events won't be deleted, but future bookings will no longer sync automatically.

## 7. Getting Help

**For questions about a specific booking** — for example, a customer's special requests, a payment query, or a rescheduling you weren't expecting — contact your manager directly.

**For technical issues with the dashboard** — for example, you can't log in, a page isn't loading, or something doesn't look right — contact Wimbledon Smart at [wimbledonsmart.co.uk](https://wimbledonsmart.co.uk). Your manager can also raise this on your behalf.

When reporting a technical issue, it helps to note what you were trying to do and what happened instead. A screenshot is always useful.
